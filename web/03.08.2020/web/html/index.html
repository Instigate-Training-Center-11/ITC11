  • Newton's Laws

Newton's three laws of motion are explained and their application to the analysis of the motion of objects in one dimension is discussed.

  • Work, Energy, and Power

Concepts of work, kinetic energy and potential energy are discussed; these concepts are combined with the work-energy theorem to provide a

convenient means of analyzing an object or system of objects moving between an initial and final state.

  • Thermal Physics

The distinction between heat and temperature is thoroughly explained. Methods of heat transfer are explained. The mathematics associated with

temperature changes and phase changes is discussed; its application to the science of calorimetry is presented.

  • Electric Circuits

The flow of charge through electric circuits is discussed in detail. The variables which cause and hinder the rate of charge flow are explained and the

mathematical application of electrical principles to series, parallel and combination circuits is presented.

  • Sound Waves and Music

The nature of sound as a longitudinal, mechanical pressure wave is explained and the properties of sound are discussed. Wave principles of

resonance and standing waves are applied in an effort to analyze the physics of musical instruments.

  • Light Waves and Color

The behavior of light waves is introduced and discussed; polarization, color, diffraction and interference are introduced as supporting evidence of

the wave nature of light. Color perception is discussed in detail.
