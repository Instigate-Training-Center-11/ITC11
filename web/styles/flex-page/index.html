Amazing natural phenomena

"Secondary rainbows are caused by double reflections of sunlight in raindrops," the encyclopedia mutters dullly. But we know that a double rainbow is just beauty squared. It is curious that in the second, less bright rainbow, the colors go in the opposite order - from the pheasant to the hunter.

The appearance of a secondary rainbow

A secondary rainbow appears outside of a primary rainbow and develops when light entering a raindrop undergoes two internal reflections instead of just one (as is the case with a primary rainbow). The intensity of light is reduced even further by the second reflection, so secondary rainbows are not as bright as primary rainbows.

Light (or solar) pillars

Light (or solar) pillar (English Light pillar) - a visual atmospheric phenomenon, an optical effect that is a vertical strip of light stretching from the Sun during its sunset or sunrise.

When the phenomenon is called?

The phenomenon is caused by the reflection of light on the nearly horizontal flat faces of hexagonal flat or columnar ice crystals suspended in air.
